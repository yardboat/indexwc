The Event

How Big a Deal Is This?

This FIFA World Cup is billed as the largest sporting event to ever happen.

That's probably going to be true.

TV audiences from all over the world will break records. Attendance should fill up NFL-sized stadiums — even despite ticket prices. More sports-event related commerce will happen than ever before.

Soccer is about to be in every imaginable space.

And hey — this is gonna be fun.

Vibe Check

But How's This Thing
Going to Feel?

Here are a few opinions, facts, and/or predictions that shed a little light on what to expect when you're expecting a World Cup.

That Soccer Bar Is Full

If you know a place as a "soccer bar," so does everyone else. And unless you're there WAY early you're not gettin' in.

America will be thirsty for insane atmospheres, and we'll all flock to places that spent the last 10 years building themselves up — like Legends, Bantam, and Smithfield Hall.

Luckily the game will be on everywhere, every time the U.S. plays. Make plans early. It's Friday night after all.

OMG It's Finally Happening

Lots of folks have wanted America to be a soccer country for a long time. The 1994 World Cup was supposed to make that happen — and in many ways it did. Participation + fandom grew by +30%.

The tournament will start with a bang, especially if the U.S. beat Paraguay. You'll see:

  • GoPro mashups of crowded bars reacting to goals
  • Profiles of foreign fanbases "taking over" cities
  • 1,000 Instagram stories of people having a time
  • Headlines: "Soccer Is Taking Over"
Call Your Soccer Friend

55 million people watched the U.S. men's last World Cup. The most-watched Premier League match of the year drew 850,000. Soccer's been growing for years — the nerdy folks at the center of it are about to get their flowers.

Finally, Some Harmony

Call me naive, but this thing still has the power to bring the world together a bit.

Be on the lookout for true feel-good moments — like when the Japanese tidied up, or when Korea put Mexico through.

Especially after the U.S. gets knocked out, there will be a ton of space to be happy together. What a novelty.

The U.S. Is Going to Impress

This team is better than everyone's thinking. In 2022 we had a good showing, falling to Belgium 3-1 in the round of 16. This group is all the same guys — and they're all WAY better — under a world-class coach.

Our thing has always been that we're not the best, but we're some dawgs that will fight and fight until we beat people we're not supposed to.

After the group stage, each successive USMNT match will be the most-watched ever.

What About the Women?

The USWNT just played Brasil twice in Brasil, then flew stateside to watch the home opener. That's an incredible experience for players like Cat Macario who've never played in a World Cup before.

The Women's Team is transitioning from Old Guard to New Guard — and the New Guard freakin' rules. They'll enter the 2027 World Cup in Brasil as favorites, bringing a whole new generation to the dance.

Something close to 30% of U.S. Soccer Fans ONLY care about the USWNT. It's never too soon to talk about their road ahead.

There's a neighborhood for every team in the World Cup. That neighborhood will go nuts when their team plays.

Go to those neighborhoods. Buy a jersey. Order a local beer. Become a Jackson Heights Colombia fan for a day, a Belgium fan at the BXL Cafe, join the Ironbound in Jersey for a Little Portugal watch party (and dinner).

The World is in New York — go find them for the most possible fun.
